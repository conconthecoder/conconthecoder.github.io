Unit 1: Columbi

A fourth of world history in one Apush Chapter

Historical outcomes

  1. Make sense of information in general and U.S. history in particular.
  2. English Civil War - King was overthrown, but his son got back into power
  3. Explain why Europeans explored the Americas, what the major Spanish explorers did, and the main characteristics of the Spanish-American empire.
  4. Describe the motivations for and characteristics of early English, French, and Dutch activities in North America.
  5. Identify the microbes, plants, animals, ideas, and people of the “Columbian Exchange” that circulated around the “Atlantic World,” and the consequences of these migrations.
  6. Define history and explain how it is put together.

Notes

  1. What is This?, Why Does it Matter?, How am I going to remember this? Recall that there are 7 themes for APUSH.
  2. The individuals Cortez (Aztec) and Columbus (America) are the two that come in mind. Also recall the encomienda system. Growing population after the black death, stronger European governments, and desire for new trade routes contributed to expansion.
  3. For England - Social (lack of land), economic (mercantilism), and Cutural (protestant religion)
    For The French/The Dutch - Money (mercantilism), traded furs
  4. Brought slaves, protestant christianity, deadly diseases, and horses.
  5. History is seen through Primary (good) and Secondary Sources (iffy)
