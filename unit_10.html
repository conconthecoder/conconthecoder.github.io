Unit 10: Civil War

Dun dun dun

Historical outcomes

  1. Explain why and how the Confederate states seceded from the Union and profile the supporters of each side.
  2. Describe the North’s advantages and challenges in the Civil War including how emancipation came about.
  3. Describe the South’s advantages and challenges in the Civil War.
  4. Assess the military leadership, naval power, and foreign policy of both sides.
  5. Analyze how the South survived through 1862.
  6. Explain why the North won the Civil War by 1865.

Notes

  1. Southerners shelled (bombed) Fort Sumner, which started the Civil War. 8 slave territories dropped out immediately, followed by 4 more (the last 4 were forced to stay in the union)
    The South was fighting for liberty, only had to stay alive while the North had to fully capture the South
  2. Lincoln was very aggressive (effective) in his policies, often manuvered around the constitution
    Many Black individuals were part of the war for the North, the North also passed a lot of bills
  3. Led by Jefferson Davis, the South was not as effective in either politcal control or finance
    Ended up printing a lot of money, crashing their economy
  4. The North had the sea advantage throughout the war
    Lincoln had bad generals after
    Southern economy was weakened because England didn't need as much cotton
    England and France remained neutral.
  5. Mclellen failed to take over Richmond because he was too timid, and got outmanuevered
    Grant was the best Union General
    Confederacy won the first battle, even though it was not a decisive one
  6. Gettysburg was the most influential battle which the union won, as it led to the Gettysburg address
    Adress claimed that the Americans were fighting for liberty and the preservation of the republic
