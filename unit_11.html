Unit 11: Reconstruction

Dun dun dun

Historical outcomes

  1. Describe the challenges of Reconstruction and how Presidents Lincoln and Johnson managed them.
  2. Detail the plans and actions of Radical Republicans to remake the South.
  3. Explain how various Southerners responded to Reconstruction.
  4. Describe the problems faced by the Grant Administration.
  5. Explain how and why Reconstruction ended.
  6. Describe the South and the position of African Americans in it during the late 1800s.

Notes

  1. Lincoln wanted to get the union states back asap, but he gets assasinated
    Still tension in the south, Black individuals wanted the right to vote
  2. Black codes - Similar to Jim Crow laws, they were laws in the South that promoted slavery
    Civil Rights Act of 1866 - Gave black people the right to vote
    The radicals really take charge, and they try to get rid of Andrew Johnson the president (fail), think that the South still doesn't have rigths
  3. Crop-Lien system - was bad, put farmers in debt (both races)
    Black land ownership increased, family structures reminiscient of the White families
    Education improved greatly in the south
  4. Grant won the election with the help of black republican voters
    Things that harmed Grant's political career
    1. Economic crisis with the Panic of 1873
    2. Corruption with taxes and the railroads (important)
    3. Currency issues
  5. The Reconstruction faded away once the black individuals got their right to vote
    The reconstruction was limited in its success
  6. The Redeemers - The new powerful class in the South
    The South industrialized a little, but they lost a lot of their education gains once their government was in power.
    Introduction of Jim Crow laws
