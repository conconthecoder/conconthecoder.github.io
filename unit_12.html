Unit 12: Westy zesty

92130

Historical outcomes

  1. Describe the peoples in the West, what they wanted, and how they got along with each other.
  2. Explain how economic activities in the West shifted in the late 1800s.
  3. Explain how artists depicted the West and how authors and historians wrote about it.
  4. Describe the policies and conflicts the U.S. government had with western Indians.
  5. Explain the opportunities and challenges western farmers faced.
  6. DBQ

Notes

  1. Very diverse workforce - Chinese (unliked), Spanish (low labor), European Immigrants
  2. Labor shortage, which means higher Labor
    Miners mined more than just gold and silver, corporations owned them
    Cattle started with free range drives, failed with a hay/food shortage for the cows
  3. Encouraged the expansion westward for all the peoples, romanticising the west and cowboys and attributing it to ideals such as freedom
  4. Initially the American wanted to concentrate the Native Americans, but later, with the Dawes Act, they wanted to assimilate them, and make them more individual rather than a collective tribe.
  5. Farming was a dying art, because demand and prices were lower, taxes were high, and Farmers were not independent
  6. DBQ - like the essay, but make sure to connect the sources and have a definitive claim
