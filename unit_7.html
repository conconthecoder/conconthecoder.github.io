Unit 7: Industrial, Exponential Growth irl

Calculus Application

Historical outcomes

  1. Describe the main population shifts of the mid-1800s.
  2. Explain the revolutions in transportation and communications by 1850.
  3. Describe why the early industrial revolution happened in the U.S. and what it meant.
  4. Explain how the industrial revolution affected laborers.
  5. Trace how American society shifted in the early 1800s.
  6. Identify how northern agricultural life changed during the mid-1800s.

Notes

  1. The Irish and the Germans were two groups who migrated
    Irish were poor, bad potato famine
    Germans were more wealthy
    Americans in general were against the people migrating in
  2. Transportation - canals (because moving items on boat was cheaper than on land) and eventually railroads
    Because of the railroads, connection between Northwest and South (no longer had to rely on the canal)
  3. This period of time including the expansion of factories, as machinery became more advanced, and also larger coporations
  4. The factory system initially made conditions worse, especially with immigrants that were willing to work for little, but workers unions helped bring the conditions to a better position
  5. Rich got richer and the poor got poorer
    Little conflict in the factories because conditions in the factories were better than those in Britian
    Middle class was growing and getting more wealthy, woman stopped working and helped around the household
  6. Farm life in general is better because of Industrial Revolution, because of more supply and also better tools (reaper and sickle)
    The Northeast steadily declined, can't compete
    The midwest, or the Old Northwest, was were the majority of farming is
