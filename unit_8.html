Unit 8: Slavery

p1

Historical outcomes

  1. Describe the economy and society of the South in the decades before the Civil War.
  2. Explain what antebellum slavery was like and how slaves resisted.
  3. Survey antebellum American literature and utopian movements.
  4. Identify the main antebellum reform movements and what they accomplished.
  5. Describe the early abolitionist movement and its limitations.
  6. Explain the value of context in understanding and evaluating history.

Notes

  1. The South's economy focused on agriculture - they intially relied on Tobacco, but it overused the land and therefore was replaced by cotton, particularly Short-Staple cotton
    South didn't progess technologically, because they thought that their agricultural system
  2. Slavery was not initially racial, but over time because the majority of slaves were black the stereotypes progressed
    Slavery got so big in America because the conditions for life were better than in the Carribean south
  3. Varied as they focused on Romanticism, which was focused more on nature rather than reason, like the Englightenment
    Promoted Westward expansion
  4. Education - Very Big W. The government focused on education infrastructure, funding, and emphasis (longer school years) Religion - W. The christians believed in self improvement, specifically going against alcohol. Science - L. The practices were still illogical, but they did teach people to wash their hands Womans Rigths/Black rights - Meh. They were eventually given, but there wasn't too much traction in the 50s.
  5. Abolitionist movements did not get that much traction in the beginning, because the Northerns were scared of the conflict that could happen
    Free soil - get rid of black people altogether.
  6. How to contextualize (not content based)
    Write in respect to what was going on at that time, emphasize (understand) not symphathize (agree)
    Don't come across as too judgemental
